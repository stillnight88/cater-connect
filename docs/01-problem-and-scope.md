# Phase 1 — Problem Understanding

> Purpose: Clearly define the real-world problem before thinking about technology, features, or implementation.

---

## 1. Problem Statement

Customers planning events in their local area lack a reliable, booking-linked system to evaluate and confidently choose catering services. Existing information is fragmented, inconsistent, and often unverified, making the decision process stressful and risky.

---

## 2. Primary User

**Primary User:**  
Customers who want to book catering services for personal or professional events within a local area.

**Secondary Users (Not Focused in This Phase):**  
Catering service providers who list and manage their offerings.

---

## 3. Core Pain Points

- Difficulty comparing caterers based on verified quality
- Reviews are scattered, biased, or not tied to real bookings
- Lack of structured booking tracking
- Fear of selecting an unreliable caterer for important events
- Excessive time spent researching and validating options

---

## 4. Why This Problem Matters

Choosing the wrong caterer can:

- Damage important events (weddings, corporate events, family functions)
- Waste significant money
- Create stress during already sensitive planning periods

A structured, booking-linked review and booking system increases trust and reduces decision friction.

---

## 5. Assumptions

The following assumptions guide the early design:

- The system initially operates within a limited local area.
- Reviews are allowed only after verified bookings.
- Caterers are willing to participate and maintain updated service information.
- Payment processing is handled outside the system (initially).

These assumptions may evolve in later phases.

---

## 6. Explicit Non-Goals (Out of Scope)

The following are intentionally excluded from the initial version:

- Mobile applications (web-only initially)
- Global or large-scale marketplace expansion
- Real-time logistics or delivery tracking
- Staff or kitchen operations management
- Advanced pricing, bidding, or auction systems
- AI-based or automated recommendations
- Payment processing and refunds

---

## Phase Completion Rule

This phase is complete when:

- The problem is clearly defined in human terms.
- The target user is identified.
- Scope boundaries are explicit.
- No technology decisions are included.
